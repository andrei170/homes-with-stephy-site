02 · Current-State Audit

Where the story leaks out

2.1 · No real website - the "site" is a stock listing feed

Her only web presence beyond social: estefaniasalazarmartinez.exprealty.com, a generic eXp IDX subdomain - no story, no branding, no Austin content, no lead capture, once defaulting to the wrong region's feed. She owns nothing searchable, branded, or lead-capturing. With 97% of buyers researching online first, the current front door works against her.

2.2 · Her best asset is buried in a Linktree

The bilingual Latina-community angle lives as one link among many, not the spine of a brand. Book Club Latinas - her signature - is a line item, not a landing page. Her strongest card is face-down.

2.3 · Traffic scatters, nothing converts

The Linktree fans visitors to four destinations - WhatsApp group, Calendly, a separate Spanish 15-min link, and a stock feed. No owned page captures an email or gives one clear next step. Visitors leak out uncaptured.

2.4 · Two booking systems and a group chat, no clear CTA

A first-timer must choose between a WhatsApp community, a general consult link, and a Spanish call link - with no guidance. Choice is friction. One warm path ("say hola") beats three good ones fighting for the same click.

2.5 · Invisible on the directories buyers use

No Zillow, realtor.com, or Google review presence found under her name. For a newer agent, an empty review footprint plus no website means a researching buyer finds almost nothing to build confidence on - right when 88% are deciding whether to trust a human with their biggest purchase.

2.6 · Entirely dependent on Instagram

Instagram is login-walled, unindexed, and rented. If the rules change or reach dips, she has no owned, searchable asset to fall back on. Everything sits on ground she doesn't control.

03 · The Positioning Opportunity

Own a lane no one else is built for

Most new Austin agents crowd the same ground: "call me for all your real-estate needs." Stephy shouldn't compete there. She should own a lane no one else is built for.

The lane

The bilingual, Latina first-time-buyer's agent - and the community that comes with her.

The lane is defensible for three data-backed reasons:

  1. The market is there and growing. ~One-third of Austin metro is Hispanic; Latinos drive ~half of US homeownership growth and a projected ~70% of net-new owners through 2040. Not a shrinking niche - the center of where the market is heading.
  2. The trust gap is real. Mortgage and contract paperwork is the hardest language barrier for Spanish-speaking buyers. An agent who removes it - line by line, in the language a family thinks in - is a relief, not a commodity.
  3. The community is a flywheel no competitor copies overnight. Book Club Latinas turns care into a loop: women join for support, learn together, become buyers and referrers. A listing feed can't do that. A relationship can.
The community flywheel, in one line

Free community brings women in → the site captures their email with a warm first-home guide → nurture and real conversations build trust → trust becomes buyers → happy buyers refer more women back into the community. The owned website is the missing hub that makes that loop turn.

04 · The 90-Day Plan

Surgical, not sprawling

Phase 1: The owned home base Weeks 1-2

  • Launch a real, owned website that leads with the bilingual, community-first brand (the concept redesign in the demo is the direction).
  • One clear primary path: "say hola" → WhatsApp or a single booking link. Retire the four-way Linktree scatter into one warm next step.
  • Bilingual throughout: English and Spanish woven together, not a separate translated page.
  • Basic on-page SEO for the searches that matter locally (bilingual Austin agent, Spanish-speaking realtor Austin, first-time buyer help).

Phase 2: One funnel Weeks 2-5

  • Build and wire the lead magnet: the free bilingual "Primera Casa / First-Home Starter Guide."
  • Name + email capture on the site, delivering the guide automatically.
  • A short, warm bilingual welcome email sequence (3-5 notes) that introduces Stephy, invites the reader into Book Club Latinas, and offers the free chat.

Phase 3: The review engine Weeks 3-8

  • Stand up her Google Business Profile and claim / complete Zillow and realtor.com so a buyer who researches her finds something.
  • A simple post-close review request flow so the proof footprint starts filling in - the thing a newer agent most needs.

Phase 4: Community-to-lead loop Weeks 6-12

  • Connect Book Club Latinas to the site so the community feeds the email list and the guide, and the site feeds the community.
  • Light content cadence (a first-home tip, a member spotlight, a market note) that keeps the loop warm.
  • Monthly one-page performance snapshot.
05 · Website Specification

The asset, tier by tier

Tier 1 - Owned home base

One self-contained, mobile-first site on a domain Stephy owns. Warm plum + rose palette, Newsreader serif, community-first voice. Sticky nav, working mobile menu, smooth reveals.

Tier 2 - Community as the spine

Book Club Latinas is built as a real feature section, not a footnote - the differentiator gets the spotlight it earns.

Tier 3 - Bilingual by design

English and Spanish side by side across hero, process steps, FAQ, and lead magnet - a Spanish-speaking visitor feels at home instantly, no translate button to hunt for.

Tier 4 - Conversion architecture

One primary CTA ("say hola," routed to her real booking / WhatsApp link), a "how it works" section that de-mystifies the journey, an FAQ answering real fears, and lead-magnet capture that turns a visitor into a contact.

Tier 5 - Trust surfaces

A values / credential bar (eXp Realty, Bilingual EN/ES, Community-first, 1-on-1 care), a warm About section, and stat tiles ready to fill with real numbers and a real Google rating as the review engine produces them. Currently placeholder-flagged, never faked.

06 · Lead-Gen Funnel

One magnet, one list, one warm path

Deliberately simple: one magnet, one list, one warm path to a conversation.

  1. Top - the magnet. Free bilingual "Primera Casa / First-Home Starter Guide": what to save, how credit and pre-approval work, first-time-buyer help many families miss, the whole journey paso a paso. Genuinely useful, exactly what her audience worries about.
  2. Capture. Name + email delivers the guide instantly and adds the contact to Stephy's owned list - the asset the Linktree throws away.
  3. Nurture. A short, warm bilingual welcome sequence introduces Stephy, invites her into Book Club Latinas, and makes the free-chat feel like an invitation, not a pitch.
  4. Convert. One next step - "say hola" - routed to her real WhatsApp / booking link, in the reader's chosen language.
  5. Compound. Buyers refer, the community grows, the list feeds itself. The website is the hub it all turns on.
