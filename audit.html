Homes with Stephy · Stephy Salazar

Austin, TX metro Prepared by AG1 Consulting July 2, 2026
Executive Summary

Stephy Salazar has the one thing that is hardest to build and easiest to lose: a genuine, warm connection to a specific community. She is bilingual English / Spanish, she is Latina, she serves the Austin metro, and she runs a real "Book Club Latinas" WhatsApp community around home ownership. In a market where roughly one-third of the metro is Hispanic and Latinos are the single largest driver of new homeownership in the country, that is not a soft asset. It is the most valuable positioning a newer Austin agent could hold.

What she does not have is anywhere to put it. Her only "website" is a generic eXp IDX subdomain (estefaniasalazarmartinez.exprealty.com) that carries no story, no branding, no lead capture, and at one pull even defaulted to the wrong region's listings. Her real personality, her community, and her booking links live scattered across a Linktree. The bilingual, Latina-community angle - the thing that makes her different from every other new agent in Austin - is her strongest card, and it is face-down.

This is not a "you are invisible, start over" audit. It is a "you already own the hardest thing, now build the asset that turns it into leads" audit. The recommendation is surgical, not sprawling: one owned website that leads with the community, one lead-capture funnel, one review engine, and a simple community-to-lead loop. The upside is unusually high precisely because she is early - the asset built now compounds across the next decade of her career, before the scattered setup calcifies into a habit.

The takeaway is not "lead with Spanish as a hard requirement." 02 · Current-State Audit

Where the story leaks out

2.1 · No real website - the "site" is a stock listing feed

Stephy's only web presence beyond social is estefaniasalazarmartinez.exprealty.com, a generic eXp IDX subdomain with no story, no branding, no Austin-specific content, and no lead capture. When pulled, it even defaulted to a listing feed from the wrong region. This is the single biggest gap: she owns nothing searchable, nothing branded, and nothing that captures a lead. For a category where 97% of buyers research online first, the current front door works against her.

2.2 · Her best asset is buried in a Linktree

The bilingual, Latina-community angle - the differentiator - lives as one link among many in a Linktree rather than as the spine of a brand. Book Club Latinas, her genuine signature, is a line item, not a landing page. Her strongest card is face-down.

2.3 · Traffic scatters, nothing converts

The Linktree fans visitors out to a WhatsApp group, a Calendly, a separate Spanish 15-minute booking link, and a stock brokerage feed. Four destinations, no single owned page that captures an email or presents one clear next step. Visitors leak out in every direction and none of them are captured for follow-up.

2.4 · Two booking systems and a group chat, no clear CTA

A first-time visitor faces a choice between a WhatsApp community, a general consult link, and a Spanish call link, with no guidance on which is for them. Choice is friction. One clear, warm path ("say hola") converts better than three good ones competing for the same click.

2.5 · Invisible on the directories buyers use

No Zillow, realtor.com, or Google review presence surfaced under her name. For a newer agent, an empty review footprint plus no website means a buyer who researches her before reaching out finds almost nothing to build confidence on - at exactly the moment 88% of them are deciding whether to trust a human with the biggest purchase of their life.

2.6 · Entirely dependent on Instagram

Instagram is login-walled, unindexed, and rented. If the platform changes its rules or her reach dips, she has no owned, searchable asset to fall back on. Everything is built on ground she does not control.

03 · The Positioning Opportunity

Own a lane no one else is built for

Most new Austin agents compete on the same crowded ground: "call me for all your real-estate needs." Stephy should not compete there. She should own a lane no one else in her market is built for.

The lane

The bilingual, Latina first-time-buyer's agent - and the community that comes with her.

That lane is defensible for three reasons the data backs up:

  1. The market is there and growing. Roughly one-third of the Austin metro is Hispanic, and Latinos drive about half of the nation's homeownership growth and a projected ~70% of net-new owners through 2040. This is not a niche shrinking into a corner; it is the center of where the market is heading.
  2. The trust gap is real. The mortgage and contract process is repeatedly cited as the hardest language barrier for Spanish-speaking buyers. An agent who removes that barrier - patiently, line by line, in the language a family thinks in - is not a commodity. She is a relief.
  3. The community is a flywheel no competitor can copy overnight. Book Club Latinas turns Stephy's care into a self-reinforcing loop: women join for support, learn about home ownership together, and become buyers (and referrers) over time. A listing feed cannot do that. A relationship can.
The community flywheel, in one line

Free community brings women in → the site captures their email with a warm first-home guide → nurture and real conversations build trust → trust becomes buyers → happy buyers refer more women back into the community. The owned website is the missing hub that makes that loop turn.

04 · The 90-Day Plan

Surgical, not sprawling

Phase 1: The owned home base Weeks 1-2

  • Launch a real, owned website that leads with the bilingual, community-first brand (the concept redesign in the demo is the direction).
  • One clear primary path: "say hola" → WhatsApp or a single booking link. Retire the four-way Linktree scatter into one warm next step.
  • Bilingual throughout: English and Spanish woven together, not a separate translated page.
  • Basic on-page SEO for the searches that matter locally (bilingual Austin agent, Spanish-speaking realtor Austin, first-time buyer help).

Phase 2: One funnel Weeks 2-5

  • Build and wire the lead magnet: the free bilingual "Primera Casa / First-Home Starter Guide."
  • Name + email capture on the site, delivering the guide automatically.
  • A short, warm bilingual welcome email sequence (3-5 notes) that introduces Stephy, invites the reader into Book Club Latinas, and offers the free chat.

Phase 3: The review engine Weeks 3-8

  • Stand up her Google Business Profile and claim / complete Zillow and realtor.com so a buyer who researches her finds something.
  • A simple post-close review request flow so the proof footprint starts filling in - the thing a newer agent most needs.

Phase 4: Community-to-lead loop Weeks 6-12

  • Connect Book Club Latinas to the site so the community feeds the email list and the guide, and the site feeds the community.
  • Light content cadence (a first-home tip, a member spotlight, a market note) that keeps the loop warm.
  • Monthly one-page performance snapshot.
05 · Website Specification

The asset, tier by tier

Tier 1 - Owned home base

A single, self-contained, mobile-first site on a domain Stephy owns. Warm plum + rose palette, Newsreader serif, community-first voice. Sticky nav, working mobile menu, smooth reveal animations.

Tier 2 - Community as the spine

Book Club Latinas is built as a real feature section, not a footnote - the differentiator gets the spotlight it earns.

Tier 3 - Bilingual by design

English and Spanish live side by side across the page (hero, process steps, FAQ, lead magnet), so a Spanish-speaking visitor feels at home immediately rather than hunting for a translate button.

Tier 4 - Conversion architecture

One primary CTA ("say hola," routed to her real booking / WhatsApp link), a "how it works" process section that de-mystifies the journey, an FAQ that answers the real fears, and the lead-magnet capture that turns a visitor into a contact.

Tier 5 - Trust surfaces

A values / credential bar (eXp Realty, Bilingual EN/ES, Community-first, 1-on-1 care), a warm About section, and stat tiles that are honest today and ready to fill in with real numbers and a real Google rating as the review engine produces them (currently placeholder-flagged, never faked).

06 · Lead-Gen Funnel

One magnet, one list, one warm path

The funnel is deliberately simple - one magnet, one list, one warm path to a conversation.

  1. Top - the magnet. The free bilingual "Primera Casa / First-Home Starter Guide": what to save, how credit and pre-approval work, first-time-buyer help many families miss, and the whole journey paso a paso. It is genuinely useful and it is exactly what her audience is anxious about.
  2. Capture. Name + email on the site delivers the guide instantly and adds the contact to Stephy's owned list - the asset the current Linktree setup throws away.
  3. Nurture. A short, warm bilingual welcome sequence introduces Stephy, invites the reader into Book Club Latinas, and makes the free-chat offer feel like an invitation, not a pitch.
  4. Convert. One clear next step - "say hola" - routed to her real WhatsApp / booking link, in the language the reader chose.
  5. Compound. Buyers refer, the community grows, and the list feeds itself. The website is the hub the whole loop turns on.

08 · Risk Reversal

Easy to say yes to

  • Own the asset from day one. The website, the domain, the email list, and the content are Stephy's - not rented from a platform or locked in a brokerage subdomain. If she and AG1 ever part ways, she keeps the whole thing.
  • Nothing faked, ever. No invented reviews, sales numbers, follower counts, or credentials. Every unverified figure stays placeholder-flagged until Stephy confirms it. Her trust with her community is the asset; it will never be spent for a shinier stat.
  • Start small, prove it, then scale. The foundation tier is a fixed, defined scope - one site, one funnel, one review engine - so the first engagement is low-risk and easy to say yes to before anything larger.
